About Me

My UX curiosity started in the World of Warcraft. I knew there had to be a way to optimize information digestion in the midst of the chaos of 40 people fighting for their lives. I wrote and distributed custom addons to help simplify spell priorities, resource costs, and other conditions to maximize moster destruction. The goal of merging stlye with maximum legibility was successful as these tools were used by tens of thousands of players including world-first teams as well as game developers at Blizzard.

Since then I've followed the call of enhancing UI & UX experiences across a variety of applications, from handling general design and front-end dev work solo, to specializing in optimizing complex cyber security workflows for multi-departmental organizations. The breadth of this background gives me advantages in understanding engineering limitations as well opportunities for efficiency, but my core motivation remains the same: to simplify everything for the user while extending their capabilities.

As far as life outside design, I live near Atlanta, GA, with my wife, two rambunctious black kittens, and two spry senior dachshunds. I'm also a competitive powerlifter, actively training and participating in local and regional events as well running a small team out of my garage gym.

Whether it's one pound or 500, fantasy foes or real cyber threats, I'm always looking to carry my part and explore new challenges.
